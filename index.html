History Of Cake

When did cakes first appear in the world?

Cakes have a rich and evolving history that dates back to ancient times. The earliest evidence of cake-like creations can be traced to the ancient Egyptians around 3000 BCE, who made honey-sweetened breads used in religious ceremonies. In Classical Greece, cakes such as "plakous," made with nuts and honey, and "satura," a heavy flat cake, became popular around the 5th century BCE. The Romans, around the 1st century BCE, also baked simple cakes from flour, eggs, butter, and honey, using them for offerings and special occasions. During the Middle Ages, cakes in Europe became more elaborate with the introduction of sugar, spices, and dried fruits. By the Renaissance, the concept of cake began to resemble what we recognize today, with advancements like refined sugar, the invention of the oven, and the use of butter and eggs. In the 18th century, the discovery of chemical leavening agents like baking soda and baking powder revolutionized cake baking, making them lighter and fluffier. Cakes became an essential part of celebrations, including birthdays and weddings. The 20th century saw cakes becoming a global phenomenon, with industrial baking and cake mixes making cake baking accessible to the general public. Today, cakes are enjoyed worldwide, with each culture contributing its unique flavors and styles to this beloved dessert.(source: See More)
